Indigenous Awl

Indigenous Awl Origin Story

A commonly used tool

Awls are a common tool recovered from Indigenous archaeological sites, and evidence of their use goes back thousands of years.

Indigenous Awl Feature Content

Made of bone

They were used to pierce holes into leather and hide, or enlarge ones that already existed. Bone was a common material used to make awls. This one is likely made from deer bone.
